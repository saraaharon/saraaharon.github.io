Cloud-Based Monitoring of Twenty Years of Shoreline Change

Ko Phi Phi Don, Thailand (2004–2024)

Multi-temporal shoreline analysis using Google Earth Engine, Landsat and Sentinel-2 imagery to quantify long-term coastal change and support sustainable coastal management.

Read Paper GitHub
Google Earth Engine Landsat Sentinel-2 ArcGIS NDWI

Project Overview

Thailand's coastal environments are under increasing pressure from tourism development and natural marine processes. This research investigates twenty years of shoreline change on Ko Phi Phi Don using cloud-based remote sensing, satellite imagery and GIS to identify long-term patterns of erosion and accretion.

Method

NDWI • Google Earth Engine

Study Area

Ko Phi Phi Don, Krabi Province

Part of the Phi Phi Islands in the Andaman Sea, Ko Phi Phi Don is a distinctive dumbbell-shaped island connected by a narrow sandy tombolo between Ao Ton Sai and Ao Loh Dalum. Its combination of limestone cliffs, coral reefs and unconsolidated sandy beaches makes it especially sensitive to coastal change.

Methodology

From Satellite Imagery to Shoreline Change

All processing was carried out on Google Earth Engine, combining Landsat imagery from 2004 and 2014 with Sentinel-2 imagery from 2024 to extract shoreline positions and compare them over time.

The Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI) was used to extract the land–water boundary for each time period. The resulting shorelines were then compared in ArcMap using a transect-based approach, with 21 transects placed around the island's perimeter.

Visual Context

Two Decades of Development

Alongside the satellite-derived shoreline analysis, ground-level photography illustrates how tourism development around Ao Ton Sai and Ao Loh Dalum has intensified over the same period.

Results

Shoreline Change, 2004–2024

Comparing extracted shorelines across all three time steps shows a clear pattern of net retreat, concentrated on the island's west, northeast and southwest-facing coasts.

Key Findings

Twenty Years in Summary

  • 117 m Maximum shoreline retreat recorded at a single transect
  • 1.01 km² Total area lost to coastal erosion between 2004 and 2024
  • 0.025 km² Total area gained through shoreline advance
  • West, Northeast, Southwest The most affected coastal sectors
Conclusion

Implications for Coastal Management

The results confirm that Ko Phi Phi Don's coastline has experienced net erosion over the past two decades, with erosion far outweighing accretion across the island. These findings support the case for targeted coastal monitoring and management to an actively retreating shoreline. The workflow itself, built entirely on freely available imagery and cloud computing, offers a low-cost, repeatable approach that could be extended to other islands facing similar pressures.
