Modeling the Spatio-Temporal Relationships between Green Infrastructure and Built-Up Density in the Urban Environment

The Case of Focșani Municipality (1990–2025)

Multi-indicator spatio-temporal analysis combining NDVI, NDBI, land surface temperature and population density to evaluate how urban expansion in Focșani has reshaped green infrastructure and the urban microclimate over more than three decades.

Read Paper GitHub
Google Earth Engine Sentinel-2 Landsat 8/9 QGIS ArcGIS Pro NDVI NDBI LST

Project Overview

Green spaces and vegetation play an essential ecological, economic, psychological, social and aesthetic role, closely linked to urban well-being. As built-up areas expand, vegetated surfaces shrink, threatening sustainability and quality of life. This research uses Geographic Information Systems to examine how urban expansion in Focșani Municipality and its ten neighbouring administrative units has influenced the distribution and continuity of green infrastructure between 1990 and 2025.

Study Area

Focșani Municipality & 10 neighbouring localities

Study Period

1990–2025

Satellite Data

Sentinel-2 ✧ Landsat 8/9 ✧ GHS-POP

Method

NDVI ✧ NDBI ✧ LST ✧ PD ✧ Google Earth Engine

Study Area

Geographical Framework and Context

Focșani Municipality lies within Vrancea County, at the contact zone between the Sub-Carpathians and the Romanian Plain. The administrative area also includes ten neighbouring localities: Odobești, Vânători, Cîmpineanca, Vârteșcoiu, Golești, Rastoaca, Milcovul, Slobozia Ciorăști, Cotești and Cârligele, which are analysed together to capture the broader dynamics of urban expansion around the municipality.

Methodology

From Satellite Data to Spatial Correlation

Four indicators were derived from three satellite data sources and processed across Google Earth Engine, QGIS and ArcGIS Pro to evaluate the spatial correlation between vegetation, built-up expansion, surface temperature and population density.

Sentinel-2 imagery was used to calculate NDVI as an indicator of vegetated areas, and Copernicus data to calculate NDBI as an indicator of built-up areas. Landsat 8/9 imagery was used to estimate land surface temperature (LST), and GHS-POP data to determine population density (PD). The spatial correlation between NDVI and NDBI, LST and PD was then evaluated to highlight the influence of built-up area expansion on vegetation and the urban microclimate.

Spatio-Temporal Dynamics

1995–2025 Overview

Between 1995 and 2025, Focșani Municipality recorded a marked imbalance between built-up expansion and green space growth, alongside a significant population decline.

Built-up areas grew by +26.61%, green spaces grew by only +7.69%, while population fell by -16.80%, a widening gap between urban expansion and green infrastructure growth.
Source: INS.

Results

Evolution of Built-Up Area Index (NDBI), 1990–2025

Change was calculated as a simple raster difference: ΔNDBI = NDBI2025 − NDBI1990.

Between 1990 and 2025, the NDBI index recorded a total increase of 59.1% across the analysed units, reflecting an intensification of urbanization. The raster difference highlights the areas of most pronounced expansion, particularly near urban centers and along major infrastructure corridors.

Results

Evolution of Population Density (PD),
1990–2025

GHS-POP data for Focșani and its ten neighbouring localities were compared between 1990 and 2025 to trace population redistribution across the study area.

The raster difference highlights a migration trend from the city center toward peripheral areas, reflecting a process of suburbanization. Golești (+204.5%) and Focșani Municipality (+119.8%) recorded the largest population gains, while Rastoaca (-12.2%) and Milcovul (-14.6%) declined.

Results

Evolution of Vegetation Index (NDVI), 2015–2024

Change was calculated as a simple raster difference: ΔNDVI = NDVI2024 − NDVI2015.

Between 2015 and 2024, NDVI recorded a significant decrease: the maximum vegetation index value dropped by approximately 0.25 units (≈29%), and the difference raster shows a predominance of negative values, down to -0.6, indicating a clear reduction in vegetation density and health.

Results

Evolution of Land Surface Temperature (LST), 2000–2024

Between 2000 and 2024, the annual average temperature increased by approximately +2°C while annual precipitation decreased by 10%, reflecting a trend toward aridification. The resulting raster indicates a decrease of up to 3.8°C in certain areas, while the center of Focșani displays minimal change (0.38°C).

Multivariate Analysis

Spatial Typologies of the Built Environment

A grouping (clustering) analysis integrated NDVI, LST, NDBI and PD values, classifying the study area into four typologies.

Key Findings

Four Cluster Typologies

  • Cluster 1 — Low thermal effect area Improved ecological conditions, with increased NDVI and decreased LST, while the built environment and population distribution remain relatively stable.
  • Cluster 2 — Consolidated urban area A process of "revegetation" or renaturalization, reflected by a strong increase in NDVI alongside declining demographic pressure and stagnation in built-up development.
  • Cluster 3 — Expanding area with thermal stress Accelerated urbanization, with very high increases in NDBI and population density; vegetation improves only slightly.
  • Cluster 4 — Residential area with reduced vegetation Significant degradation of vegetation cover within a context of urban and demographic stability.
Conclusion

Implications for Urban Planning

The study demonstrates the usefulness of integrating remotely-sensed indices (NDVI, NDBI, LST, PD) for analyzing urban spatio-temporal relationships within a GIS environment. Green areas decreased significantly between 1990 and 2025 due to urban expansion as well as the pedological drought during 2020–2022, and human pressure continues to affect the urban environment and quality of life. A significant imbalance is observed between green and built-up areas, highlighting the need to expand green spaces. The main driver of built-up expansion and, consequently, the reduction of green surfaces is the road network; the development of the A7 Motorway is expected to intensify this dynamic in the coming years by increasing accessibility and stimulating investment in adjacent zones.
